Endocrine Hormones Defined

Endocrine hormones are chemical messengers released by glands directly into the bloodstream. They travel to distant target cells, bind to specific receptors, and trigger coordinated physiological changes.

  • Secreted in very small amounts
  • Specific receptor binding determines target response
  • Negative feedback loops stabilize internal conditions
